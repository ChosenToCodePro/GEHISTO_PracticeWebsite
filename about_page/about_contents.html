About

We have compiled information related to the works of Antonio Pigafetta, a Nobleman
and a Scholar who had ventured with Magellan across the seas in search of spices.
This site includes information about The First Map of the Strait of Magellan of 1520,
Pigafetta’s account of the First Mass in the Philippines, and the Battle of Mactan, all
laid out in a modernized website using imagery inspired by the aesthetics of
skeuomorphism. Bask in the presence of a snapshot, a view in history that invites you
to know more of what you might not know.

About Pigafetta’s Journey

Tracing the voyage through three major events of 1521

The First Map of the Strait of Magellan (1520)

Pigafetta’s map depicted the Strait of Magellan—an essential, yet treacherous sea passage discovered during the first circumnavigation of the globe.
Although dangerous, it became a crucial route linking the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans before the Panama Canal existed.

Pigafetta’s Account of the First Mass in the Philippines

The first Catholic Mass was held on March 31, 1521 in Limasawa, as documented by Antonio Pigafetta. Father Pedro de Valderrama
officiated the Mass, which was attended by local leaders and Magellan’s crew, marking the formal introduction of Christianity in the Philippines.

The Battle of Mactan

On April 27, 1521, Lapulapu and his warriors successfully repelled Magellan’s forces in Mactan. Despite their advanced weapons,
the Spaniards were defeated due to local resistance and the terrain—an act recorded by Pigafetta that represents early Filipino bravery.
